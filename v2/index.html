The trust gap is the product.

Black and Hispanic adults use culturally trusted voices for health decisions at rates 50–75% higher than white peers. This is not an anomaly — it reflects decades of structural provider mistrust, geographic access gaps, and historically poor representation in healthcare marketing.

No existing telehealth platform is built around cultural trust transfer. Hims, Ro, and Hers operate mass-market performance-advertising funnels — they do not structurally address the demographic gap.

The SPEAK Act (February 2026) and FDA compounding enforcement era close the door on the generic peptide arbitrage that funded competitors' early growth. The structural moat shifts from acquisition cost to cultural credibility.

02 · The Model

Founders, not faces.

The deal architecture is consistent across all sub-brands. Cultural partners are equity stakeholders in their own LLC — compensated through distributions, milestone bonuses, and a $500K–$1M personal signing payment at execution — not brand license fees or talent retainers.

Entity Structure

Dedicated LLC per sub-brand

Each partner operates through their own entity (e.g., Cardi B Wellness LLC), a wholly-owned subsidiary under OpenLoop Creator Brands LLC, managed by Obsidian Capital Partners. The partner earns through LLC distributions — not as talent on payroll — creating a fundamentally different incentive structure than endorsement.

Compensation · Day 1

$500K–$1M signing payment

Paid personally at execution — not deferred, not milestone-gated. This signals seriousness, distinguishes Commonground from pure endorsement deals, and establishes immediate alignment. Partners with larger existing patient-facing audiences and higher cultural healthcare trust scores command the upper tier.

Equity & Distributions

Equity stake + milestone bonuses

Partner holds equity in their sub-brand LLC and earns through distributions as the LLC scales. Personal milestone bonuses unlock at defined patient enrollment and monthly revenue thresholds, aligning long-term engagement with operational outcomes.

Launch Capital

$150K launch fund per sub-brand

OpenLoop provides $150K at launch for brand production, content seeding, and early paid-acquisition support. Once the LLC reaches self-funding threshold via subscription revenues, paid acquisition migrates entirely off the OpenLoop balance sheet.

Self-Funding Flywheel

LLC subscription revenue → paid acquisition

Target: $10M/month at maturity, funded entirely by LLC subscription revenues. This creates a capital-efficient path to scale and eliminates OpenLoop's continued outlay beyond the initial launch period. The LLC becomes self-sustaining before the growth-phase funding requirement triggers.

OpenLoop Return

Equity stake + Membership Services spread

OpenLoop earns its equity position in each LLC plus the spread between the patient subscription price and the Membership Services Fee paid to OpenLoop for clinical infrastructure access. Aligned incentive: more patients = larger aggregate spread = greater OpenLoop return.

06 · Risk & Compliance

Compliance as competitive moat.

The enforcement environment that is a headwind for underprepared competitors is a structural tailwind for Commonground. Building compliance into the architecture — not bolting it on after launch — is the differentiator that earns institutional partner confidence.

High Variable · Managed

Celebrity / Partner Variable

The primary structural risk is partner-driven controversy. Governance framework: controversy clause triggers a 90-day cure period. Unresolved: LLC distributions suspended pending board review. A pre-agreed buy-back mechanism allows OpenLoop Creator Brands LLC to acquire partner equity at a defined valuation formula — protecting the sub-brand's clinical continuity regardless of individual partner status. The LLC structure ensures the clinical business survives partner events.

Regulatory · Active Enforcement

FTC Claim Substantiation

All health claims in partner content undergo pre-publication review by OpenLoop's clinical team. FTC material-connection disclosure is contractually required on every post, regardless of platform or format. The FTC v. NextMed ruling (2024) and ongoing GLP-1 advertising scrutiny make this a live enforcement environment. Commonground treats disclosure infrastructure as a launch-day requirement, not a post-enforcement remediation item.

Regulatory · Structural Shift

FDA Compounding Posture

The SPEAK Act (February 2026) and FDA's enhanced enforcement on compounded GLP-1s have significantly narrowed the competitive landscape for underprepared operators. Commonground's program strategy anticipates this: GLP-1 represents 30% of Cardi Care's program mix, with HRT, female sexual health, and NAD⁺ providing diversification. OpenLoop's formulary management team navigates compounding compliance as part of the standard infrastructure agreement.

Access · Strategic Opportunity

Multilingual & Cultural Access

Black and Hispanic adults comprise the core target demographic across all four sub-brands. Access architecture must reflect this:

  • Spanish-language intake and clinical consultation available at launch
  • Clinician diversity prioritized in OpenLoop cohort assignment per sub-brand
  • Partner voice ensures cultural fluency at the top of the acquisition funnel — not a UX patch
  • Payer enrollment targets community health plan coverage common in target demographics
Operational · Standard Management

Clinical Quality & Outcomes

OpenLoop's enterprise track record — 170+ integrations, 5× YoY growth — provides baseline confidence in clinical quality at scale. Sub-brand-specific outcomes monitoring begins at launch. Patient satisfaction, adverse event tracking, and program dropout rates feed quarterly reviews with Obsidian Capital Partners. Outcomes data supports milestone bonus verification and informs program mix evolution.

Financial · Scenario-Modeled

Revenue Model Sensitivity

The moderate (base case) scenario assumes ~12% monthly churn and a two-phase enrollment model (organic M1–6; self-funded paid acquisition M7–24). Key sensitivities: (a) partner launch campaign performance and earned media conversion, (b) program mix churn differential, (c) payer enrollment ramp speed. Conservative and optimistic bounds are presented in each scenario table. No scenario assumes external capital injection beyond the $150K launch fund per sub-brand.

Compliance as Moat

Every competitor weakened by FDA compounding enforcement, FTC scrutiny, or post-pandemic trust erosion represents a market share transfer to operators who got compliance right from Day 1. Commonground's advantage is not just clinical infrastructure — it is the combination of cultural trust (the acquisition lever no ad budget can replicate at this demographic depth) and institutional governance (the retention and continuity lever no endorsement deal can sustain alone). The moat is cultural × structural — and each element reinforces the other.
